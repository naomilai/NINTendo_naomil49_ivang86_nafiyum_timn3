Though social anxiety can absolutely affect people of many different backgrounds and situations, it is important to notice that a person's individual demographics can affect their life experience. In turn, this can make them more susceptible or likely to experience high levels of anxiety. The dataset we used has a number of demographic identifiers labeled along the data such as age, gender, and occupation.

Age

The age of participants in any survey is always a crucial factor to consider. When looking at and analyzing data, or even setting up a survey, you have to judge what kind of sample will be represented in your survey (one specific age group or a broad number of them). But even more important, in this dataset, it would be important to understant how different ages can be affected by social anxiety. The participants in the dataset ranged from 18 to 64 years of age. The mean age is 40.2, and while there are several peaks at certain age ranges (18, 26, 32, 42, 48, 56, 63) of around 700 participants, there are fair amounts of answers in the other in between ages of around 520 participants.

Gender

Another important aspect of an individual's quality of mental health is how thoroughly it is maintained. Therapy is one of the leading and most beneficial ways to ensure overall quality of life, by dealing with mental health challenges and strong emotions. In our dataset, participant data is held on whether there is any history of anxiety within their family by "True" and "False". Overall, across the data roughly 53%, or 5847 individuals, answered true to having a family history of anxiety.

Occupation

Though anxiety is a disorder that stands separate from others, both in possible causes but also in steps taken to help treat it, other factors, specifically similar mental health issues, can very often be corresponding and relating to the prevalence of anxiety. In our dataset, participant data on their stress level is obtained with a participant self-judged rating of how stressed they are in their life on a scale of 1 to 10. In general, there was a fairly even distribution of responses from 1 to 7, of around 1000 participants per each of those ratings, with another set of consistency from 8 to 10, with around 1350 particpants identifying themselves among each of those ratings. Though somewhat leaning towards the higher values, the mean ends up at 5.86, which still makes it a fairly even and representative set of data.
